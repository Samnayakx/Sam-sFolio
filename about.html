A product designer building at the frontier of AI — turning raw capability into experiences that feel human, intuitive, and inevitable. I work where careful craft meets the ability to ship.

I came up through fashion, through graphic design, through years of obsessing over the details nobody notices but everyone feels. That foundation shapes everything I make — systems that hold up, interfaces that breathe, products people reach for without thinking.

Traveling

Moving through unfamiliar places resets my perspective. Every new city, coast, or mountain trail sharpens how I see space, people, and the small details that make design feel human.

Video Games

Games taught me more about feedback loops, progression, and delight than most design textbooks. I still believe the best UX lessons come from a well-designed level.

Fashion Design

Fashion is where I first understood that design is never just visual — it's tactile, temporal, and cultural. Those years shaped how I think about texture, identity, and narrative in everything I make.

Graphic Design

Before screens, there were grids, type, and ink. Graphic design gave me a rigorous foundation — the kind that makes you obsessive about alignment, hierarchy, and whitespace.

Creative Coding

Code is my second sketchbook. From generative art to interactive prototypes, I use it to explore ideas that static tools can't hold — and to build the things I can only imagine.
