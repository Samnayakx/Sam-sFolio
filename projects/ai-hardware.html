Overview

How might fashion enterprises turn complex product workflows into agentic systems?

CotonAI is an agentic platform for fashion enterprises, focused on AI-powered workflows that help teams create, review, and operationalize product content in fast-moving environments.

Problem

People already record pieces of their lives, but the pieces rarely become useful.

Notes, screenshots, photos, voice memos, calendar events, and chat messages all hold context. The problem is retrieval: people need the right reminder at the right time, not another archive to manage.

Fragmented capture

Useful signals are spread across apps and formats, so context disappears when a task moves.

Weak recall

People remember the feeling of a moment but not the names, links, decisions, or next steps.

Trust gap

Always-on recording creates social discomfort unless control and feedback are visible.

Solution

A wearable that captures intentional moments and makes them actionable later.

The experience centers around moments: compact summaries with participants, places, media, decisions, and suggested follow-ups. The device is only valuable when the app makes memory easy to inspect and control.

1. CapturePress, speak, or gesture
2. UnderstandSummarize private context
3. ActPrompt useful next steps

Core Flows

Designing the moments loop from capture to recall.

In-the-moment prompt

The device suggests a concise action without pulling the user into a full screen.

Moment timeline

The app organizes captured context by people, location, and task so recall starts from a familiar cue.

Review detail

Each moment shows source material, generated summary, confidence, and privacy controls.

Onboarding

The first run teaches what is captured, when recording is active, and how to delete data.

Research

Privacy and social signals shaped the product more than the hardware.

Competitive research showed that tiny devices often fail when they optimize for novelty over trust. I mapped wearable form factors, capture modes, battery constraints, and social cues to identify where the interface needed to do the most work.

Wearable pinFast capture, high visibility
PendantNatural form, lower camera angle
Phone companionLess novel, easier trust

Design Decisions

The final direction traded perfect memory for user control.

Manual capture first

Intentional capture makes behavior easier to understand for both the wearer and people nearby.

Visible rationale

Every prompt explains why it appeared, using plain cues like person, place, or unfinished task.

Delete at source

Users can remove raw media and summaries from the same detail view without hunting through settings.

Reflection

What I learned

The strongest interaction was not the futuristic one. It was the one that made capture obvious, recall fast, and consent visible. A memory product only works when people understand what the system knows and why it is speaking up.

Good AI UX turns uncertainty into clear, reversible choices.
