Overview

How might fashion enterprises turn complex product workflows into agentic systems?

CotonAI is an agentic platform for fashion enterprises, focused on AI-powered workflows that help teams create, review, and operationalize product content in fast-moving environments.

Image placeholder

AI Studio product overview and workspace entry point.

Problem

Fashion product teams need more than isolated AI tools.

Product workflows move across briefs, references, product data, reviews, and operational handoffs. When AI appears as a standalone tool, teams still have to translate outputs back into the messy systems where decisions actually happen.

The design challenge was to make agentic behavior legible enough for enterprise teams to trust, inspect, and reuse.

Flow placeholder

Current fragmented workflow and AI trust gaps across product creation.

Solution

An AI workspace that turns product context into guided enterprise workflows.

The experience centers on workflows rather than prompts. Teams can bring product context into the system, let agents generate or transform assets, and review outputs with clear rationale and control.

The product is most useful when AI actions feel inspectable, reversible, and connected to the next operational step.

Core Flows

Designing the loop from product context to reviewed output.

The core flow moves from capturing business context, to agent-assisted generation, to review and operational handoff. Each step makes the system's assumptions visible so teams can correct or approve work without losing momentum.

Research

Enterprise AI UX depends on trust, control, and workflow fit.

Discovery focused on how product teams evaluate AI output quality, where they need control, and which decisions require visible rationale. The strongest patterns were less about novelty and more about confidence, traceability, and reuse.

Image placeholder

Research themes, workflow maps, and trust/control findings.

Design Decisions

The final direction traded magic for clarity.

The interface makes agentic actions feel understandable by showing inputs, generated outputs, and next-step controls in one workflow. Rather than hiding complexity, the system gives teams enough context to decide whether the output is useful.

This direction supports rapid prototyping while keeping enterprise review needs visible.

Reflection

What I learned

The strongest AI interaction was not the most autonomous one. It was the one that made system behavior clear enough for teams to trust, correct, and operationalize.

Good AI UX turns uncertainty into clear, reversible choices.

Designing AI products, systems, and interfaces with care.
