Overview

How might drivers get clearer routes, job status, and handoffs in one mobile flow?

Gatson Driver App is a driver-first mobile experience for route clarity, job status, and delivery handoffs, designed around fast decisions in operational contexts.

Problem

Practice data is easy to collect and hard to interpret.

Learners often know whether a session felt good, but not what changed or what to do next. Existing tools either over-indexed on stats or gave generic advice that did not connect to recent behavior.

Low signal

Raw numbers did not explain what caused improvement or regression.

Weak habit loop

Users needed a reason to return after a session ended.

Generic advice

Recommendations felt interchangeable when they ignored recent history.

Solution

A session review that turns performance into one focused next step.

The product reframes feedback around trends, short explanations, and one recommended exercise. Instead of a dashboard of everything, users get a tight loop: review, understand, practice again.

Core Flows

Each flow reduces the distance between insight and action.

Session summary

A plain-language recap explains what changed since the previous session.

Pattern view

Trends are grouped by behavior, not just metric category.

Recommended drill

The app picks one exercise based on the highest-leverage gap.

Progress reflection

Users can mark whether a recommendation felt accurate to tune future guidance.

Research

Interviews showed that motivation dropped when feedback felt final.

Learners wanted feedback that felt like coaching, not grading. That shifted the interface away from scores as the hero and toward small, next-session improvements.

Design Decisions

The final experience emphasizes direction over judgment.

One recommendation

Too many next steps made users less likely to start again.

Plain explanations

Metrics are translated into coaching language before showing details.

Positive trend framing

The UI surfaces improvement before gaps to keep motivation intact.

Reflection

What I learned

Feedback products need emotional pacing. The best version did not show less information; it sequenced information so users felt ready to act on it.

A useful coach makes the next rep feel obvious.
