Overview

How might drivers get clearer routes, job status, and handoffs in one mobile flow?

Gatson Driver App is a driver-first mobile experience for route clarity, job status, and delivery handoffs, designed around fast decisions in operational contexts.

Video placeholder

Mobile driver experience overview and route handoff context.

Problem

Operational work breaks down when route context, job status, and handoffs live in separate places.

Drivers need to make fast decisions while moving through changing routes, job states, and delivery handoffs. When the interface forces them to interpret scattered information, each step adds uncertainty.

Flow placeholder

Current driver workflow showing route, job status, and handoff friction.

Solution

A mobile flow that makes the next operational action obvious.

The product reframes the driver experience around route clarity, plain-language job status, and focused handoff actions. Instead of a dashboard of everything, drivers get a tight loop: understand the route, complete the task, confirm the handoff.

Image placeholder

Driver home, active route, and task detail screens.

Core Flows

Each flow reduces the distance between status and action.

The core flows support route review, job progress, exception handling, and delivery handoff. Each screen keeps the driver oriented around what changed, what needs attention, and what action comes next.

Flow placeholder

Route review, active job, exception handling, and delivery confirmation flow.

Research

Drivers need feedback that feels like direction, not extra admin.

Research themes pointed toward lightweight guidance, gentle progress visibility, and avoiding overcorrection. The interface needed to support operational confidence without becoming another system to manage.

Image placeholder

Research themes and field workflow notes.

Design Decisions

The final experience emphasizes direction over judgment.

The interface favors one clear next action, plain explanations, and positive progress framing. The goal is to keep drivers moving while still making status and accountability visible.

Prototype placeholder

Final mobile prototype for route, job status, and handoff flows.

Reflection

What I learned

Operational products need emotional pacing too. The best version did not show less information; it sequenced information so drivers felt ready to act on it.

A useful operations tool makes the next step feel obvious.
