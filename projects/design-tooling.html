Overview

How might Trailhead adapt for advanced users after they reach major career milestones?

Salesforce Trailhead is an interactive learning platform designed to help users build practical Salesforce skills through guided modules and hands-on challenges. As users evolve into experts, their engagement shifts from continuous learning to more sporadic, goal-driven usage.

This project explores how Trailhead can adapt to support advanced users and keep them meaningfully engaged over time.

Problem

Advanced users disengage after certifications and return only when a specific problem appears.

After users reach key milestones, Trailhead becomes less of a daily learning habit and more of a reactive resource. The core gap is that Trailhead does not evolve with the user's career stage.

Flow placeholder

Engagement drop, external-tool reliance, and personalization gap mapped across the current journey.

Solution

Reimagine Trailhead as a career-driven, adaptive learning ecosystem.

The direction shifts Trailhead from a static learning platform into a system that helps users solve real problems while continuing to grow their careers.

A career-aware dashboard shows progress, skill gaps, certifications, benchmarks, personalized learning paths, and next steps. Trailhead Solve adds a RAG-powered AI layer for direct, contextual answers grounded in community, documentation, and learning resources.

Core Flows

The experience moves users from asking real questions to building long-term career momentum.

The user begins with a real-world Salesforce question instead of browsing static learning paths. Solve returns step-by-step answers from trusted sources, then suggests next actions based on skill gaps and assembles custom Trailmixes.

Certifications, progress, and opportunities stay visible so users keep engaging beyond milestones.

Research

Power users preferred fast problem-solving over structured learning.

The research combined 11 power user interviews, 6 competitor platform analyses, semi-structured interviews, and behavioral insights. Engagement was tied to immediate utility, career relevance, and visibility into progress and next steps.

Image placeholder

Power user interview themes, competitor analysis, agent stress tests, and think-aloud findings.

Design Decisions

The final direction prioritizes intent, trust, and career relevance.

The experience prioritizes intent over navigation so users can ask before they browse. The primary behavior shifts from browsing content to asking for grounded help, with progress and skill gaps visible enough to reinforce habit loops.

RAG keeps answers grounded in documentation, community, and learning resources, while agent-based testing helped stress-test flows before external validation.

Reflection

What I learned

This project reframed learning from a content consumption problem to a career progression problem. By introducing an AI-driven problem-solving layer and a career-aware system, Trailhead becomes less of a learning platform and more of a daily utility.

If extended further, the opportunity lies in deep personalization at scale, stronger community integration, and continuous feedback loops to refine AI accuracy.
