Overview

How might Trailhead help learners solve, practice, and retain platform skills?

Trailhead Solve focuses on research and design for guided learning workflows, including research operations across 650+ users and a shortlist of 72 participants for deeper study.

Problem

Desktop creation patterns do not shrink cleanly to a phone.

Small screens punish scattered controls, hidden state, and modal-heavy flows. Users can make quick changes, but they lose confidence when they cannot compare versions or understand what is selected.

Selection ambiguity

Users struggled when object selection, canvas movement, and edit mode competed for the same gestures.

Control overload

Full toolbars created visual noise and pushed the actual work area out of focus.

Version anxiety

People hesitated to experiment when they could not quickly compare or revert changes.

Solution

A compact editor built around modes, previews, and reversible actions.

The prototype uses segmented editing modes, persistent before/after comparison, and predictable bottom-sheet controls. The goal is to make the current task feel obvious without hiding power behind menus.

Core Flows

Four flows carry the editing experience.

Open and crop

The crop frame has stable handles and predictable snapping, so layout never jumps under the finger.

Adjust style

Sliders and swatches sit in one bottom sheet, with live preview above the controls.

Compare versions

A hold-to-preview interaction shows the original without leaving the edit context.

Export

Presets make output constraints explicit before saving or sharing.

Research

Testing showed that confidence mattered more than raw feature count.

I compared mobile editors and watched people make a simple social asset. The fastest users were not the ones with the most tools; they were the ones who always knew what state they were in.

Design Decisions

The final design keeps the canvas visually dominant.

Bottom-first controls

Primary controls stay within thumb reach while the work stays visible.

One active mode

Mode labels reduce accidental edits and make gesture behavior predictable.

Preview as habit

Comparison is available in every mode, not buried in history.

Reflection

What I learned

A mobile design tool should feel narrower, not weaker. Constraints helped clarify what belonged in the primary flow and what could wait until users needed more precision.

Small screens reward interfaces that make one action feel unmistakable.
