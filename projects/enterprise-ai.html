Overview

How might 3D product visualization improve fit confidence before purchase?

At Gap Inc., this work focused on improving 3D and fit experiences, supporting AR Try-On initiatives, and helping reduce ecommerce returns through clearer product visualization.

Problem

Internal knowledge existed, but it was hard to trust under pressure.

Operators needed fast answers while handling live requests. The old workflow required searching multiple repositories, checking freshness, and rewriting findings into a ticket or message.

Slow lookup

People lost time translating a customer question into the right internal keyword.

Source doubt

Even when an answer looked right, teams needed proof before acting on it.

Messy handoffs

Escalations often missed the reasoning that led to a decision.

Solution

An answer workspace that treats confidence as part of the UI.

The assistant pairs generated answers with highlighted source passages, freshness signals, and an escalation summary. The design avoids a black-box chat experience by making the system's evidence visible.

Core Flows

The workflow supports answering, checking, and transferring context.

Question intake

Users can paste a messy request and the system extracts the likely policy intent.

Evidence review

Answers are shown next to source snippets, dates, and document owners.

Follow-up prompts

The assistant suggests clarifying questions when the source is incomplete.

Escalation packet

A concise handoff includes the question, answer, citations, and remaining uncertainty.

Research

Shadowing sessions revealed that the real job was decision support.

I observed operators handle repeated questions and mapped where they paused. Most delays happened after retrieval, when they needed to determine whether an answer was current enough to use.

Design Decisions

We designed the assistant to slow down at the exact moments risk increased.

Citations by default

No answer appears without visible source context.

Uncertainty states

The UI clearly separates confirmed answers from partial matches.

Reusable summaries

Operators can transfer a clean explanation into tickets without rewriting.

Reflection

What I learned

For enterprise AI, speed is only useful when trust keeps up. The strongest design choices made evidence, uncertainty, and ownership visible in the same place as the answer.

Trustworthy AI interfaces show their work before users have to ask.
